Each row gives the average of a measurement for one subject at one activity. These are given by the numbers 1-30 in the Subject column
and the 6 different possible activities (Walking, Walking Upstairs, Walking Downstairs, Sitting, Standing, Laying) in the Activities
column. The other 79 columns are various measurements collected in the experiment.

The t or f at the beginning of a variable name indicates whether it is a time or frequency domain variable, respectively. The next few
letters after "Body" indicate what the variable is measuring (for example, Acc for accelerometer, Gyr for gyroscope). The few letters
after that indicate whether the variable is measuring mean ("mean") or standard deviation ("std"). Finally, the letter at the end of the
variable name indicates what axis is being measured (X, Y, or Z).\
